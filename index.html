About Dublin

Dublin, the capital city of Ireland is situated on a bay on the east coast, at the mouth of the River Liffey, it lies within the province of Leinster. It is bordered on the south by the Dublin Mountains, a part of the Wicklow Mountains range. Dublin is booming with tech giants calling the Docklands home and new hotels, pubs and restaurants sprouting throughout the city. Despite all the busynesss, Dublin retains its village-like charms, with historic neighborhoods reminders of gentler, slower-moving times. Whether you like your city breaks with a modern sheen or with an other-era luster, Dublin has something for you. You’ll never run out of things to do and see in Dublin City – there’s just so much happening all year round.

Upon your visit, we implore you to discover Dublin culture, from the famous Guinness Storehouse to St. Patrick's Cathedral, you can take a walk back in time to learn about Irish heritage and events that have shaped Dublin's history. Many attractions around the city offer self-guided tours and guided programmes for visitors to learn about the rich history and legends of the site. Many also host annual events, festivals, and other activities each season for a richer cultural experience.

City Center Attractions

Trinity College Dublin

Visit this esteemed research university and the home of the Book of Kells, the sole constituent college of the University of Dublin.

Glasnevin Cemetary

Opened in 1832, it holds the graves and memorials of several notable figures who have helped shaped Irish history and culture.

Temple Bar

Temple Bar is located opposite the River Liffey in central Dublin. It is promoted as Dublin's 'cultural quarter' and, as a centre of Dublin's city centre's nightlife.

Dublin Castle

Erected in the early thirteenth century on the site of a Viking settlement. It is now a major government complex and a key tourist attraction.

Guinness Storehouse

Guinness Storehouse is a tourist attraction at St. James's Gate Brewery in Dublin, Ireland. Since opening in 2000, it has received over twenty million visitors

Outdoor Activities

Phoenix Park

The Phoenix Park is the largest enclosed public park in any capital city in Europe. The main gates of the Park are open 24 hours.

Howth

Fresh sea breezes most days. Great views of the rugged coastline and cliffs.

Poolbeg Lighthouse

The Poolbeg Lighthouse Walk is a gorgeous coastal walk that’ll blow off the thickest of cobwebs.

National Botanic Gardens

Ireland's premier garden is a green oasis situated in the leafy suburb of Glasnevin, not far from Dublin city centre.
